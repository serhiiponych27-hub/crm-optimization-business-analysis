# Executive Summary

## Project Overview

This project analyzes and proposes improvements to the CRM sales process used by a design and renovation company with approximately 50–70 employees.  
The company operates with a mixed sales model combining inside sales managers and field sales specialists and serves both B2B and B2C clients.

The typical sales cycle includes the following stages:

Consultation → Estimation → Proposal → Contract

The CRM system is currently used by sales managers to track leads and opportunities; however, it functions primarily as a record-keeping tool rather than an operational management system.

---

## Problem Statement

The current CRM usage does not provide sufficient operational control over the sales pipeline.

Key issues identified:

- lack of pipeline discipline
- inconsistent stage transitions
- poor data quality
- unclear ownership of leads and opportunities
- inconsistent follow-up with potential clients
- absence of structured analysis of lost deals

Because of these issues, the CRM system cannot provide reliable analytics or support effective sales management.

---

## Business Impact

The problems described above create several business risks:

- unreliable revenue forecasting
- limited visibility into sales performance
- loss of potential clients due to delayed follow-ups
- difficulty scaling the sales team
- lack of actionable insights into reasons for lost opportunities

As a result, management cannot effectively monitor sales performance or identify areas for improvement.

---

## Proposed Solution

The proposed solution focuses on introducing governance and structure into the CRM sales process.

Key improvements include:

- standardized sales pipeline stages
- validation rules for stage transitions
- required fields for critical data points
- automatic lead assignment
- structured tracking of lost deal reasons
- SLA monitoring for follow-up activities
- implementation of KPI-based reporting dashboards
- role-based access control

These changes transform the CRM from a passive database into an operational sales management system.

---

## Expected Benefits

Implementing the proposed improvements is expected to provide the following benefits:

- increased transparency of the sales pipeline
- improved data quality in the CRM
- more accurate revenue forecasting
- faster response time to potential clients
- improved accountability among sales managers
- ability to analyze and reduce lost opportunities

Overall, the optimized CRM process will support better decision-making and improve the company's sales performance.
